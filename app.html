    FAQ & about the science

    What each test measures, what it categorically can't do, and why this is built the way it is.

    What does it actually measure?

    Three cognitive tasks and two physiological proxies:

    • PVT (psychomotor vigilance) — reaction time and sustained attention; lapses are the classic fatigue signature.
    • Stroop — response inhibition / cognitive control, from the slowdown on incongruent colour-word trials.
    • 2-back — working memory, scored as d′ (sensitivity), so guessing doesn't inflate it.
    • Heart rate & RMSSD proxy — from PPG inter-beat intervals. On PPG this is strictly pulse-rate variability, so it's labelled "proxy" throughout.
    • Electrodermal arousal — from the GSR sensor. It helps separate fatigue-like dips from elevated sympathetic activation, but it is not emotion detection.
    What can it not do?

    It does not measure cerebral blood flow, brain activity, or anything neurological. It is not a medical device: no diagnosis, no medical advice. Scores are relative, heuristic indicators for self-tracking and product exploration — useful against your own history, not as a clinical number.

    What's the "temple" placement, and why is it capped?

    An experimental forehead/temple sensor position. Even there, PPG reflects superficial skin perfusion only — not brain hemodynamics. It is noisier and unvalidated, so the app lowers data confidence for temple sessions rather than overclaiming. It's included, clearly labelled, because honest exploration beats a hidden limitation.

    Why an active test instead of passive tracking?

    Passive wearables infer state while you do nothing. An active check measures the thing you actually care about — current performance — directly, and gives a clean, repeatable baseline-vs-load comparison in a few minutes. It's a deliberate instrument reading, not an ambient guess.

    What is the data-confidence score?

    The score that gates all the others. It folds in signal quality, motion-clean fraction, task completion, and placement mode. A readiness number from a bad signal is worse than no number, so the interface shows when it distrusts its own data — the live console desaturates and the waveform turns amber the moment perfusion drops or motion is detected.

    Where does my data go?

    Nowhere. Everything is stored locally in your browser (IndexedDB), per person. There is no account and no upload. You can export or import your history as JSON for backup or device migration from the History page. Storage is unreliable on file://, so the hosted (and installable) version is the real home for a stateful, daily product.

    Can I install it?

    Yes — it's a PWA. On the hosted version your browser will offer "Install" (or "Add to Home Screen"); it then runs offline with its own icon, no app store, no fee. The double-click-the-file demo still works for a quick look, just without reliable storage.
